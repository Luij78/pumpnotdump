## What Makes a Great Real Estate CRM?

Before diving into specific platforms, let's establish what actually matters:

**1. Lead Capture & Organization**
Your CRM should pull leads from Zillow, Realtor.com, your website, and social media ads into one dashboard. No more checking five different inboxes.

**2. Automated Follow-Up**
80% of sales happen after the 5th contact. A good CRM sends drip emails, texts, and reminders automatically so no lead falls through the cracks.

**3. Transaction Management**
From contract to close, you need checklists, document storage, and deadline tracking. The best CRMs handle this natively.

**4. Mobile Experience**
You're showing houses, not sitting at a desk. Your CRM must work seamlessly on your phone.

**5. Integration with Your Stack**
MLS, email marketing, calendar, e-signature tools — your CRM should connect to everything you already use.

## How to Choose Your CRM

Ask yourself these questions:

1. **What's your budget?** Solo agents can start with Wise Agent ($49/mo). Teams should budget $100-500/mo.

2. **Do you need transaction management?** If yes, choose Wise Agent or BoldTrail. If not, Follow Up Boss wins on lead management.

3. **How tech-savvy are you?** Simpler platforms like LionDesk have easier learning curves. Follow Up Boss and kvCORE require more setup time.

4. **What integrations matter?** Check that your must-have tools (Zillow, Gmail, DocuSign) connect before committing.
