## Chapter 2: Real Estate Laws and Rules (14 Questions — PASSED ✅)

### Q1. After a real estate transaction closes, who is the only person that may pay the licensee for real estate services?
- **(c) Employing broker** ✅
- **Why:** A sales associate may ONLY receive compensation from their employing broker. Not from the seller, buyer, or attorney. Getting paid directly by a client = operating as broker while licensed as sales associate = violation.

### Q2. What does Florida Statute 475.23 require a real estate licensee to do upon changing employing brokers?
- **(d) Licensees must provide notice to FREC within 10 days after the change** ✅
- **Why:** F.S. 475.23 requires notification within 10 days for employer/address changes. Failure = $500 fine. Key numbers: 10 days for employer/address, 30 days for criminal convictions.

### Q3. Which type of broker relationship requires licensees to provide the specific duties of confidentiality, obedience, loyalty, and full disclosure?
- **(d) Single agency relationship** ✅
- **Why:** Single agency = full fiduciary duties (COLDA: Confidentiality, Obedience, Loyalty, Disclosure, Accounting). Transaction broker = limited duties. No brokerage = no representation.

### Q4. Which statute regulates Florida real estate licensees, schools, and appraisers?
- **(b) Chapter 475** ✅
- **Why:** F.S. 475 = THE real estate statute. F.A.C. 61J2 = admin rules (not a statute). Chapter 455 = general professions act. Chapter 718 = condos.

### Q5. Once a sales associate receives a deposit from a buyer, he/she must give it to their employing broker during what time frame?
- **(b) By the end of the next business day** ✅
- **Why:** Deposit checks → broker by end of NEXT business day. Monday receipt = Tuesday deadline. Not 3 days, not 24 hours, not "when offer is accepted."

### Q6. Which shows the penalties for violations of Florida real estate license law in order of least to most severe?
- **(c) Fine, suspension, revocation** ✅
- **Why:** Full order: Reprimand → Fine → Probation → Suspension → Revocation/Denial. Only (c) lists three penalties in correct ascending order.

### Q7. All of the following actions would be a violation of F.A.C. 61J2-10.025 as it applies to advertising, EXCEPT:
- **(d) Including the brokerage name adjacent to, immediately above or below "point of contact" information** ✅
- **Why:** EXCEPT question. Placing brokerage name next to contact info = proper compliance, not a violation. All ads must include brokerage firm name prominently.

### Q8. What happens to a sales associate that does not complete their Sales Associate Post-License requirements (1st renewal) before the renewal deadline?
- **(d) License becomes null and void** ✅
- **Why:** Post-License (1st renewal) failure = null and void. Must start completely over. This is HARSHER than regular CE non-compliance. Key distinction from Q13.

### Q9. Which Florida Administrative Code authorizes the FREC to enforce rules against its members?
- **(b) 61J2** ✅
- **Why:** Question says "Administrative Code" not "Statute." F.A.C. 61J2 = FREC's rules for RE licensees. 475 & 455 are statutes (F.S.). 61J1 = appraisers.

### Q10. Licensees, in providing real estate services to the public, are bound to provide the following duties in all brokerage relationships, EXCEPT:
- **(a) Loyalty** ✅
- **Why:** EXCEPT question. Loyalty is ONLY owed in single agency (COLDA). Transaction brokers don't owe loyalty — just honesty, disclosure, accounting, and fairness.

### Q11. Which brokerage relationship situation is not permitted under Florida laws and rules?
- **(b) Representing a buyer with limited confidentiality in a single agency relationship** ✅
- **Why:** Single agency = FULL confidentiality. "Limited confidentiality" = transaction broker duty. Can't mix single agency with limited duties — contradiction not permitted.

### Q13. After the 1st renewal, what is the license status if the licensee fails to complete their 14 hours of CE by the 2nd renewal date?
- **(c) Reverts to involuntarily inactive; 12 months to complete CE and pay late fee** ✅
- **Why:** Regular CE failure (2nd+ renewal) = involuntarily inactive + 12 months grace. Post-License failure (1st renewal) = null and void. BIG difference.

### Q14. Licensed sales associates must report all of the following changes to the Department, EXCEPT:
- **(a) Report when their broker no longer holds deposit monies and now transfers them to a title company or attorney** ✅
- **Why:** Must report: employer change (10 days), address change (10 days), criminal conviction (30 days). How broker handles escrow = broker's business, not a reportable change.

## KEY CONCEPTS TO MEMORIZE

### Notification Deadlines
- Change of employer: **10 days**, $500 fine
- Change of mailing address: **10 days**, $500 fine
- Criminal conviction: **30 days**

### Penalty Order (least → most severe)
1. Reprimand → 2. Fine → 3. Probation → 4. Suspension → 5. Revocation/Denial

### License Renewal Consequences
- **Post-License (1st renewal) failure** = NULL AND VOID (start over)
- **Regular CE (2nd+ renewal) failure** = Involuntarily inactive (12 months to fix + late fee)

### Brokerage Relationships
- **Single Agency** = Full fiduciary (COLDA: Confidentiality, Obedience, Loyalty, Disclosure, Accounting)
- **Transaction Broker** = Limited duties (limited confidentiality, honesty, fairness, disclosure, accounting — NO loyalty/obedience)
- **No Brokerage** = No representation

### Key Statutes
- **F.S. 475** = Real estate licensees, schools, appraisers
- **F.S. 455** = General regulation of professions
- **F.S. 120** = Administrative Procedures Act
- **F.A.C. 61J2** = FREC admin rules for RE licensees
- **F.A.C. 61J1** = Appraisers

## Chapter 3: Evaluating and Pricing Property

### Section 1 Quiz (8 Questions — PASSED ✅)

### Q1. In the early 1980s, banks and S&Ls went through instability. Part of the responsibility was placed on whom?
**✅ Appraisers** — Inflated appraisals led to bad loans during the S&L crisis, leading Congress to create FIRREA.

### Q2. What term refers to a RE financial transaction that FFIRA has contracted for or regulates, and requires an appraiser?
**✅ Federally related transaction** — The key definition from FIRREA/Title XI.

### Q3. To correct problems during the collapse of lending institutions, Congress created what?
**✅ Financial Institutions Reform, Recovery, and Enforcement Act of 1989 (FIRREA)** — The other options (NBA, REAP, R.E. 475) are fake.

### Q4. Title XI of FIRREA required appraisals in federally related transactions be in conformity with what?
**✅ Uniform Standards of Professional Appraisal Practice (USPAP)** — The other acronyms (USPTA, FHSAA, ABCD) are made up.

### Q5. A licensee must never refer to a CMA as what?
**✅ An appraisal** — CMAs use appraisal concepts but may NOT be called an appraisal.

### Q6. An appraiser conducts an independent, impartial, and objective analysis based on research and data pertaining to the value of what?
**✅ The specified real property** — Not similar properties or previous appraisals. The SPECIFIC property.

### Q7. All of these are valid reasons for an appraisal EXCEPT which one?
**✅ Property lien evaluations** — Valid reasons: federal estate taxes, eminent domain, insurance claims, investment planning, federal income tax claims.

### Q8. Under what condition may a RE licensee who is NOT a state certified appraiser perform an appraisal?
**✅ The valuation is not to be used in a federally related transaction** — Licensees CAN appraise for non-federal transactions only.

### Section 2 Quiz (5 Questions — PASSED ✅)

### Q1. The comparable sales approach is based on the principle of substitution. Which statement best demonstrates it?
**✅ Among 3 comparable properties, the one with the lowest list price attracts the most demand due to buyers not wanting to pay more than necessary.** — Other options: A=Change, B=Progression, D=Anticipation.

### Q2. Tim and Sue have the smallest home in a sought after neighborhood. Value increased because of higher value homes. Which principle?
**✅ Progression** — "Worst house on the best block" — value goes UP.

### Q3. Which type of value is assigned by the property appraiser for ad valorem tax purposes?
**✅ Assessed value** — Assessed = ad valorem tax. Investment = specific investor. Liquidation = failing business. Insured = insurance payout.

### Q4. Which term best describes the amount actually paid in a RE transaction?
**✅ Price** — Price = actually paid. Market value = what it SHOULD sell for. They're different!

### Q5. A seller remodels the kitchen, adding $30,000 to value. What is this $30,000 called?
**✅ Contribution value** — It's the value the improvement ADDS to the property, not what it cost.
